What's a knot?

A knot is a 3D loop (like a rubber band). However, these loops can get tangled to points of no return! Below we have two different knots, can you untangle the knot on the right to look like the one on the left without breaking the loop?

The answer is no! These two knots are different and cannot be deformed to look like each other. In fact there are plenty of knots that cannot be deformed to look like others. Here are a few examples.

These are known as prime knots.

Knots can look different

We can alter how a knot looks by stretching, squeezing, or twisting it, so long as the loop doesn't break.

Gif sourced from: cantorsparadise.com

So how can you tell two different types of knot apart, or which knot is which?

This is where knot theory comes in! Knot theory is a branch of mathematics that studies knots.

Mathematicians have introduced many different measures for discerning between knots, these are known as knot invariants.

Physicists have particular interest in knots and knot invariants as they come up in a variety of places, from DNA to Quantum mechanics.

The problem is that there is no known knot invariant that can uniquely describe every knot.
